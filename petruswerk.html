As a housing company of the diocese of Berlin, the Petrus plant was founded in 1958 by Bishop Julius Döpfner. To this day, one follows the core idea: to act economically, to act socially - to shape contemporary living spaces. Since 2004, the petrus werk belongs to the AVILA Group .


HISTORY

On August 8, 1958 as a result of the German Catholic Conference, Cardinal Julius Döpfner has petrus factory founded as a social institution of the Diocese of Berlin. Pressured by the distress, uncertainty of the post-war period and a deep crisis of divided Berlin, the newly built housing and settlement business of the diocese begins to build apartments for a broad section of the population.

In 1989, new perspectives opened up for the petrus factory, The fall of the Wall, the abolition of borders, offered the opportunity for economic engagement in the eastern part of Berlin and in Brandenburg. The petrus werk acquired building land as well as buildings in need of modernization and renovation for its assets, which were beyond the former West Berlin borders. On these areas, condominiums developed in the following years, whose residential units could be successfully sold. At the same time, numerous rental apartments in the former eastern part of the diocese were taken over by the administration.

A new era of the Petrus factorybegins in 2003 with the takeover of the company by Dr. med. Douglas Fernando led AVILA Holding. The Petrus factory became a subsidiary of the Group with the core business of managing its own and third-party owner-occupied and rental apartments.
